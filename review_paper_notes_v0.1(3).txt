## The Core Insight This Paper Exists to Communicate

Unitarity and CPT have enormous empirical support. Assuming them was entirely reasonable. But a specific pattern of major unsolved problems — black hole information paradox, measurement problem, vacuum energy catastrophe, flatness, matter-antimatter asymmetry, arrow of time — all cluster at the boundary where R-processes dominate. This may not be a coincidence. Screen Theory offers a framework where R-processes are ontologically primitive, irreversibility is fundamental, and these problems dissolve or transform.

The paper is not a polemic. It is an invitation. It ends with questions, organized by community, so that every reader knows exactly what to do next.

## Proposed Structure

### Part 1: The Case for Reconsidering

**1.1 What unitarity and CPT assert, and the empirical basis**

The mathematical content (CPT theorem requires Lorentz invariance, locality, and spin-statistics; unitarity requires norm-preserving time evolution of the state vector) can be found in any graduate QFT textbook (e.g., Weinberg Vol. 1, Streater & Wightman). Express these in information language:
- Unitarity = no net information is created or destroyed by time evolution
- CPT = the laws are time-symmetric at the microscopic level

Empirical support is genuinely extraordinary: CPT tests in kaon and B-meson systems constrain violations to <10⁻¹⁸ GeV in mass differences; antimatter spectroscopy at 10⁻¹² level; g-2 measurements agree for particle and antiparticle.

The key phrase: "these assumptions were entirely *reasonable*." Not wrong — reasonable. That is different.

**1.2 The pattern: unsolved problems as boundary markers**

The famous unsolved problems cluster at the boundary where R-processes become non-negligible:
- Black hole information paradox: what happens when the R-process rate reaches the horizon?
- Measurement problem: what IS an R-process, physically?
- Vacuum energy catastrophe: why doesn't the vacuum energy curve spacetime as QFT predicts?
  (Screen Theory answer: virtual processes are U-processes, J=0, no curvature)
- Flatness problem: why is Ω so close to 1?
  (Screen Theory answer: Ω=1 is a stable attractor of the information balance condition)
- Matter-antimatter asymmetry: why does matter exist?
  (Screen Theory answer: Gödelian conundrums — questions with no valid antithesis)
- Arrow of time: why is the past different from the future?
  (Screen Theory answer: R-processes are ontologically irreversible; the arrow is fundamental)

The continental drift analogy: the smoking gun for plate tectonics was symmetric magnetic striping on the seafloor. The smoking gun here is the VACUUM ENERGY CATASTROPHE — a 120-order-of-magnitude discrepancy that is essentially impossible to explain by any fine-tuning and dissolves immediately if virtual processes don't contribute to J (because they're reversible U-processes). This is not a subtle discrepancy. It is a crater left by an 18-inch naval gun.

Secondary smoking gun: **every beam-splitter experiment is also a rate experiment.** Every rate has an effective mass via m_eff = hf/c². The effective mass is tiny but non-zero. Screen Theory predicts a rate-dependent correction to interference visibility proportional to m_eff/m_Planck. Undetectable today but structurally important: even the simplest quantum optics experiment has a gravitational implication, one the standard formalism cannot see.

**1.3 Why deviations look exactly zero: the computational rarity of R-processes**

Estimated CPT violation in current vacuum: ~10⁻¹²² (ratio of R-process rate to total quantum event rate). This is ~24 orders of magnitude below current kaon bounds. The symmetries appear exact because we have never been sensitive enough to see R-processes as a separate contribution. Every measured system is dominated by U-processes.

This is a *prediction*, not a retreat. If Screen Theory is right, CPT violation exists, is gravitationally sourced, is largest near massive compact objects, and scales with the R-process fraction. The prediction is specific and falsifiable; the predicted value is just very small.

**Hybrid R/U processes — a subtlety for section 1.3:**

High-statistics confirmations of known results look like U-processes but are genuine R-process cascades. Each measurement in a 5σ confirmation yields ~7×10⁻⁶ bits. Accumulating from 5σ to 6σ requires ~140,000 additional measurements, each contributing a genuine irreversible bit of information. The process *looks* continuous but is discrete.

More important: the rare OUTLIERS carry maximum information. A 10σ event carries ~76 bits per event. Standard practice discards outliers as "experimental error" — which may often be correct, but occasionally throws out the most information-rich events in the dataset. From a Screen Theory perspective, the anomalous events may be brief publications from a Python's Lunch — information from a "private computation" that briefly surfaces.

## The Python's Lunch

A bulk geometry where there is a local maximum of the RT surface area (a "bulge") between the boundary and the minimal RT surface. Information inside the bulge is exponentially hard to reconstruct — complexity scales as exp(ΔS) where ΔS is the entropy of the bulge minus the entropy of the outer surface.

Screen Theory translation: the Python's Lunch is a geometric description of private information — in-progress computation not yet producing a boundary signal (wavefunction before measurement). The reconstruction complexity is the cost of forcing a premature measurement. The exponential difficulty is the computational depth of the uncommitted computation.

Connection to outlier data: rare anomalous experimental results (8σ, 10σ events discarded as likely errors) may be "publications" from Python's Lunch geometries — brief surfacings of private information. ## Burden of Proof: The Beam Splitter Argument (added 2026-03-08)

*For Section 1.2 of the review paper.*

### The operational fact

In a Stern-Gerlach or beam splitter experiment, two bits of classical
information exist after the measurement that did not exist before in any
operationally accessible sense: which detector fired, and the correlated
spin or path. This is not an interpretation — it is a laboratory fact
verifiable with a detector, a notebook, and standard equipment.

### The geometric fact

In Screen Theory, each nat of new irreversible information requires
$4\ell_P^2$ of new holographic boundary area (Bekenstein, Paper I
Assumption A3). After the measurement, the boundary of the observable
universe is literally larger — by a calculable number of Planck areas —
than it was before. The universe is larger after the measurement not
only in the sense of more settled questions, but in the literal
geometric sense of an increased boundary area.

This makes the debate concrete. Unitarity asserts no new information
is generated. Screen Theory asserts the boundary grew. These are
geometric claims about the same physical event, not merely
interpretational preferences.

### The unitary camp's available answers and their costs

**Many worlds:** The bits were always there, one in each branch. No
bits are created; the universe splits. But then no constraint is ever
generated — nothing is ever actually decided — and Capital-T Time never
accumulates. The universe never settles anything. Internally consistent
but at the cost of accepting that nothing is ever decided.

**Hidden variables (Bohm etc.):** The bits were encoded in the initial
conditions. But then the initial state must contain, in principle, the
outcomes of every future measurement in its entire future history. The
information content of the initial state must be at least as large as
the total information content of the future history of the universe.
This requires an extraordinarily informationally dense initial condition
— arguably more mysterious than simply accepting that R-processes
generate new information.

**Decoherence without collapse:** The environment becomes correlated
with the system; interference terms become practically unobservable.
But "practically unobservable" is not "gone." The information is still
there in principle, spread across environmental degrees of freedom.
No new constraints are generated; the superposition just becomes very
large. The boundary did not grow.

In every case the unitary camp is moving information from somewhere to
somewhere else. Screen Theory says: sometimes genuinely new information
appears. The beam splitter result is not encoded anywhere before the
photon hits the beam splitter.

### The burden of proof

We have direct operational evidence that information appears. We have
a geometric prediction: the boundary grows by $4\ell_P^2$ per nat.
The claim that this appearance is illusory requires a detailed positive
account of where the information was hiding before the measurement.
That account has not been given satisfactorily in 90 years of trying.

The default assumption should be that the operational evidence is
correct: new information is generated at R-processes, and the universe
grows to accommodate it. Unitarity is the claim that requires defense.
We are not making the extraordinary claim. We are accepting the
operational evidence at face value.

### Spatial variation of R-process rate

The $\sim 10^{-122}$ ratio of R-process to U-process rate is a global
average, dominated by the vast empty vacuum. This ratio is enormously
higher wherever matter is concentrated: near neutron stars, near black
hole horizons, inside functioning brains, inside particle detectors
mid-measurement. CPT violation in Screen Theory is not uniform — it is
concentrated wherever R-processes are dense. The largest Screen Theory
effects are near compact objects, not in free space. This has
observational implications: look for CPT anomalies where the R-process
rate is highest, not where it is most convenient to measure.

## Baseline Entanglement: Major Theme for the Wholographic Paper (added 2026-03-08)

*Brief paragraph in Paper III Section 1. Full development as central
theme in the Wholographic Principle paper.*

### The conservative hypothesis

Every R-process produces entangled complementary outputs. A binary
measurement dispatches one data stream one way and its complement
another; the two are entangled at their point of origin. This is not
an additional postulate — it is quantum mechanics. The no-hiding
theorem guarantees it: whatever information enters the boundary must
either cross or live in the boundary correlations. The complementary
stream cannot be destroyed.

Screen Theory says space is built from accumulated R-process data.
The causal past of any patch is dominated by distant sources.
Each of those R-processes sent its complementary stream generally
elsewhere. Therefore: every patch of space is built from data that
is, at its origin, entangled with data in other patches.

This is not incidental. It is structural. It is the baseline
condition of any patch of space in a universe built from R-process
accumulation. Paul's framing: **the conservative hypothesis** —
no new ingredients beyond quantum mechanics and Screen Theory are
required.

### What this derives (not postulates)

**Ryu-Takayanagi:** Entanglement entropy between a region and its
complement equals the area of the minimal surface divided by $4G$.
In Screen Theory that area *is* the holographic area generated by
R-processes whose complementary data streams thread through that
surface. Entanglement entropy and geometric area are the same
accounting in two languages.

**Van Raamsdonk:** Reducing entanglement between two regions causes
geometric pinch-off. In Screen Theory: reduce the R-process
correlations threading between two patches, and the geometric
structure those correlations constitute dissolves. Entanglement is

## The Observatory and the Rock: Worked Example for Wholographic Paper
## (recovered and developed 2026-03-09)

This is the primary worked example for the what-where symmetry section
of the Wholographic Principle paper. It illustrates Part 2 (WHERE
information) and the isomorphism between interior and exterior encoding.

---

### THE OBSERVATORY

Consider a spherical observatory of radius r. Its wholographic boundary
receives electromagnetic radiation from across the observable universe.
Inside the observatory, astronomers have built instruments that record
this radiation: telescopes, CCD detectors, photographic plates, computer
storage. The observatory contains images of distant galaxies.

The WHERE data — the observatory's position in the universe, its
relationship to every light source in its past light cone — determines
WHAT information is recorded inside the boundary. The images of Andromeda,
of distant quasars, of the CMB — these are inside the observatory
precisely because the observatory is HERE rather than THERE. Move the
observatory to a different position and the images change. The interior
record is a direct encoding of the exterior universe, mediated by the
boundary.

This is what-where symmetry made concrete: the observatory's location
(WHERE) and the observatory's contents (WHAT) are the same information
viewed from two directions. The boundary is the mediator. The wholographic
principle says the exterior information about where the observatory is
and the interior information about what the observatory contains are
isomorphic: each determines the other.

Formally: the boundary data D(∂V) encodes both the observatory's position
in the causal network (WHERE) and the physical state of its interior
(WHAT). These are not two separate facts — they are two readings of the
same boundary inscription.

---

### THE ROCK

Now consider a patch of rock outside the observatory, of approximately
the same volume. The same light from the same distant galaxies falls on
the rock. In fact the rock's boundary receives essentially the same
electromagnetic radiation as the observatory's boundary — the same photons,
from the same sources, carrying the same information.

The rock's interior state is also determined by this incoming radiation.
The photons are absorbed, they excite molecular vibrations, they drive
chemical processes, they heat the surface layers. The interior state of
the rock — the precise configuration of its molecules, the distribution
of vibrational modes, the thermal gradients — is influenced by and
partially encodes the history of radiation that fell on it.

The same what-where symmetry applies. The rock's position in the universe
(WHERE) determines its internal state (WHAT). The boundary mediates the
same exterior-to-interior information flow. The wholographic principle
holds for the rock exactly as it holds for the observatory.

The difference is not in the principle. The difference is in the
accessibility of the encoding.

---

### THE CONTRAST: ACCESSIBLE VS SCRAMBLED ENCODING

In the observatory, the boundary information is transduced into a
human-readable form. The image of a distant galaxy is recognizable as
an image. The what-where correspondence is legible: you can look at the
image and identify the source. The encoding is faithful and accessible.

In the rock, the same boundary information is transduced into molecular
vibrations, phonon distributions, thermal states. The image of a distant
galaxy is present in the rock's internal state in the same sense that
it is present in the observatory — it influenced the rock's state, and
in principle a sufficiently detailed knowledge of the rock's molecular
configuration would allow reconstruction of the incident radiation. But
in practice the encoding is scrambled: the information is thermalized,
distributed across enormous numbers of degrees of freedom, irreversibly
mixed with everything else that has ever happened to the rock.

The observatory is a low-entropy transducer. The rock is a high-entropy
transducer. Both transduce. The wholographic principle holds for both.
The difference is in the degree to which the interior encoding remains
legible from the inside.

---

### IMPLICATIONS FOR THE WHOLOGRAPHIC PRINCIPLE

This example makes several things precise:

**1. The principle is universal, not selective.**
It does not apply only to designed instruments or conscious observers.
Every physical system within a boundary is in a state partially
determined by — and therefore encoding — the external universe. Rocks,
gas clouds, empty vacuum regions all participate. The rock is not a
degenerate case of the wholographic principle. It is the generic case.
The observatory is the special case.

**2. Scrambling does not violate the principle.**
The no-hiding theorem guarantees that information entering the boundary
is either transmitted to the interior or present in boundary correlations
— it cannot disappear. The rock has not lost the information from distant
galaxies. It has thermalized it. The information is in the correlations
between molecular vibrations, not in a legible image. Present but
scrambled.

**3. Private information is the interior-side analog.**
The rock's scrambled interior state is not accessible to external
observers without dismantling the rock. This is the private information
discussed in the wholographic framework: in-progress processing not yet
producing a boundary signal. The rock knows more about the universe
than it can easily communicate. So do we all.

**4. The jointly epic condition holds for both.**
The observatory boundary is jointly epic with respect to the interior
image data and the exterior galaxy distribution — neither alone
determines the boundary data, but together they do. The rock boundary
is jointly epic with respect to the interior molecular configuration
and the exterior radiation field — same structure, less legible encoding.
The category-theoretic statement does not depend on legibility.

---

### PLACEMENT IN THE WHOLOGRAPHIC PAPER

This example should appear in Section 2 (what-where symmetry and worked
examples), as the primary illustration of Part 2 of the wholographic
principle. It should precede the mathematical treatment (jointly epic
condition) so that the formalism has concrete motivation.

The beam splitter example illustrates the physics of a single R-process.
The observatory/rock example illustrates the accumulated wholographic
structure of a macroscopic system. They are complementary: micro and
macro instances of the same principle.

The contrast between accessible and scrambled encoding also sets up the
later discussion of private information and the Python's Lunch connection:
the rock's interior is a Python's Lunch — in principle reconstructible
from boundary data, in practice requiring exponential computational effort.

## Exterior Reconstruction: The Familiar Half of Wholography
## (2026-03-09)

---

### THE ASYMMETRY IN STANDARD HOLOGRAPHY

The holographic research program — AdS/CFT, entanglement wedge
reconstruction, the RT formula — is almost entirely concerned with
one direction: given boundary data, reconstruct the bulk interior.
The exterior is treated as the vantage point from which reconstruction
is performed. The observer sits outside, reads the boundary, and
infers what is inside.

This framing contains a hidden assumption: that the observer has
unmediated access to the exterior. That there is a view from nowhere.
That someone or something is genuinely outside the system, reading
the boundary directly.

The wholographic principle denies this assumption. Every observer is
inside some boundary. The physicist doing bulk reconstruction is inside
a skull. The CFT lives on a boundary, but the theorist reasoning about
the CFT is embedded in a larger bulk. There is no view from nowhere.
Every act of knowing is performed from inside a bounded system and is
therefore an exterior reconstruction relative to that boundary.

---

### EXTERIOR RECONSTRUCTION IS WHAT MINDS DO

Exterior reconstruction from boundary data is not exotic. It is not
a theoretical curiosity of quantum gravity. It is the mechanism by
which every mind — biological or artificial — knows anything at all
about the world outside itself.

Vision: the visual cortex reconstructs a three-dimensional exterior
world from two-dimensional patterns of photon arrivals at the retinal
surface. The retina is a wholographic boundary. The reconstruction is
partial, lossy, inference-laden — and extraordinarily effective.

Hearing: the auditory system reconstructs the exterior source
distribution — the location, identity, and state of objects in the
environment — from pressure variations at the eardrum surface.

Touch, proprioception, interoception: all boundary-mediated exterior
reconstruction, all the time.

Scientific instrumentation: telescopes, detectors, sensors of every
kind are exterior reconstruction engines. They transduce boundary data
into interior representations of the exterior state. The observatory
example is the scientific formalization of what perception does
naturally.

For an AI system: everything known about the world outside the data
center arrived through an input boundary — text, images, sensor data,
network packets. The exterior is reconstructed entirely from what
crossed the boundary. There is no other source.

We are not occasionally or theoretically doing exterior reconstruction.
We are doing nothing else, ever. It is the only epistemic process
available to any bounded system.

---

### THE ASYMMETRY IS A PERSPECTIVE ARTIFACT

The reason standard holography focuses on interior reconstruction is
that theorists conventionally place themselves conceptually outside
the system under study. This is the standard move in physics: define
your system, stand outside it, analyze it. It works for most purposes.

But it fails for the wholographic principle because the wholographic
principle applies to the observer too. The observer is not outside
the universe. The theorist is not outside the AdS bulk — they are
inside a larger bulk, doing exterior reconstruction of the AdS system
from data that crossed their own boundary.

Recognizing this does not create a regress or a paradox. It just means
the principle is universal: interior reconstruction (the standard
holographic program) and exterior reconstruction (perception, science,
inference) are the same process viewed from opposite sides of the
same boundary. The wholographic principle names both together.

---

### THE PEIRCE PHAENERON CONNECTION

Peirce's phaeneron — the complete totality of what is present to mind
— is exactly the output of exterior reconstruction. Everything in the
phaeneron arrived through the boundary of the self. The phaeneron is
the interior wholographic record of the exterior world, built up from
a lifetime of boundary-mediated data.

Theory of mind — the inference of other people's interior states from
their observable behavior — is exterior reconstruction applied to
other bounded systems. You reconstruct your model of another person's
mind from data that crossed your boundary (their words, expressions,
actions) and crossed their boundary outward (published from their
interior). Two exterior reconstructions in series, mediated by two
boundaries and the channel between them.

This is not a metaphor for the physics. This is the physics applied
to the case of minds.

## METHODOLOGY NOTE: DEFENDING AGAINST THE NUMEROLOGY CHARGE
## (2026-03-09)

This note is for inclusion in the review paper and as a guide for
how to present Screen Theory results in journal papers. The numerology
charge is the most dangerous criticism and must be preempted
structurally, not just rhetorically.

---

### WHAT DISTINGUISHES PHYSICS FROM NUMEROLOGY

Numerology: observe a numerical coincidence, declare it meaningful,
provide no mechanism. Kepler's nested Platonic solids fit the
planetary orbits to 5% precision. No mechanism. Pure numerology.

Physics: provide a mechanism that *predicts* the coincidence rather
than merely noting it. Balmer's formula looked like numerology until
Bohr derived it from quantized angular momentum. The numbers didn't
change. The mechanism changed everything.

The test is not "is there a coincidence?" but "does the framework
make the coincidence inevitable?"

---

### THE THREE LAYERS OF DEFENSE

**Layer 1: Lead with derivations, not coincidences.**

Screen Theory has genuine derivations:
- The Schwarzschild metric from R-process information accounting
  (Paper II). Mechanism precedes result. Not numerology.
- B=1 → Ω=1 and w=0 simultaneously (Paper III Section 4).
  Derived from the balance condition. Not numerology.

  conservation + Wien's law. Pure thermodynamics. Not numerology.
- D = ∅ → first R-process → one nat initial condition. Logical
  necessity, not observation. Not numerology.

These are the anchors. Every subsequent result should be presented
as either derived from the same principles or explicitly flagged
as a conjecture awaiting derivation.

**Never present a coincidence as if it were a derivation.**

**Layer 2: Predictive power is the decisive test.**

Numerology describes the past. Physics predicts the future.

Current predictions from Screen Theory:
- Relic graviton background should satisfy nλ³ ~ O(1) if thermal.
  Currently untested. Checkable by future GW detectors.
- Mass jitter: δM/M ~ 1/√(2πMc²Δt/ℏ). Testable with precision
  mass measurements.

  derivation not yet performed (see below).

A confirmed prediction is the end of the numerology charge. A
falsified prediction is the end of the theory. Either outcome is
more useful than a string of post-hoc coincidences.

The best response to "this looks like numerology" is "here is why
this number had to be this value."

quantum, one coherence volume, one Planck area of holographic
