## Vacuum Catastrophe Reframed (Paper III / synthesis)

Standard picture: QFT predicts ~10^122 vacuum fluctuations per unit
volume contributing to the cosmological constant; observed value is
120 orders of magnitude smaller; therefore something cancels to
implausible precision. This is treated as a fine-tuning catastrophe.

Screen Theory answer: those 10^122 transitions ARE happening. They are
the computation running along the past light cone, coordinating the
next moment's classical output with the entire accumulated history of
the universe. Nearly all are reversible — they generate no curvature,
leave no geometric trace. Only the irreversible R-processes that produce
a new classical fact contribute to the cosmological constant.
The ratio of irreversible to total transitions is ~10^-122.
This is not a cancellation. It is a selection.
The cosmological constant is not a fine-tuning problem.
It is the measure of the universe's irreversibility rate.

The computation is CONSTRAINED: every output must be consistent with
existing history while leaving room for future history to develop.
Every point on the past light cone is running part of the same
calculation and the outputs must mesh. This coordination is why the
universe doesn't get stuck in logical contradictions.

## Gravity Wells as Logjams (Paper III / IV)

Deep in a gravity well, incoming information is so dense and so
historically entangled that pushing into the future requires more
computational work per unit of new information produced.
High curvature = high coordination cost = logjam.
The event horizon is where the coordination cost equals the production
rate — the surface where publication rate equals question-arrival rate
(from Screen Theory Core document).

This connects conundrum cosmology to the vacuum computation picture:
conundrums (galaxy cluster seeds, SMBH seeds) are the places where
four incompatible information domains meet and the coordination cost
is highest. They are the universe's hardest computational problems,
expressed geometrically as the most curved regions.

## Domain Topology, Worldlines, Events, and the Four-Color Theorem

### The Hierarchy (4D spacetime)

In a generic domain decomposition of 4D spacetime:

- 3D bulk: 1 domain (voids — informationally quiet, low curvature)
- 2D walls: 2 domains meet (cosmic walls — questions being processed)
- 1D filaments: 3 domains meet (compound questions)
- 1D worldlines: 4 domains meet — PERSISTENT through time → PARTICLE
  (4-domain junction is generic and robust in 4D; it has a stable identity)
- 0D Events: 5 domains meet — INSTANTANEOUS → R-PROCESS
  (5-domain meeting is codimension-4 coincidence, measure zero, cannot persist)

The distinction between worldlines and Events IS the distinction between
U-process and R-process.
- 4-domain worldline: unitary evolution, particle identity carried forward
- 5-domain Event: irreversible, instantaneous, classical output produced

Matter particles are where 4 domains meet. This explains why R-processes
happen at matter particles: a worldline (4-domain junction) that intersects
another worldline creates a 5-domain meeting → Event → R-process.

### Engineering and Probability

In a randomly assembled domain structure in 4D:
- 4-domain meetings: GENERIC (expected at vertices of Voronoi-like
  decomposition in 3+1 dimensions)
- 5-domain meetings: require ONE additional coincidence (two worldlines
  intersecting) — less common but not rare; drives all of physics
- 6-domain meetings: require TWO additional coincidences — extraordinary;
  possibly corresponding to multi-particle interaction vertices
- N-domain meetings: require N-5 additional coincidences; increasingly
  improbable but engineerable

### The Four-Color Theorem Connection

The four-color theorem: in any planar map, four colors suffice — you never
NEED a fifth region mutually adjacent to four others in 2D.

The reason 5-domain meetings require global information to resolve is
exactly the reason the four-color theorem requires a global proof:
local rearrangements can always defer the problem but cannot eliminate it.
Appel and Haken (1976): irreducibly required checking 1,936 cases —
the proof is global and computational, not local and elegant.

If the Screen Theory domain structure has the same character, then
R-processes (5-domain Events) are precisely situations where local
information is INSUFFICIENT to determine the outcome and global
coordination across the past light cone is required.

This is a beautiful characterization of quantum measurement:
the outcome cannot be determined locally. The whole picture is needed.

Topology of the surface matters: the four-color theorem applies to
planar maps (sphere topology). Different topology → different theorem
→ possibly different physics for non-trivial spacetime topologies.
Worth investigating for Paper IV (black holes, non-trivial topology).

### Intergalactic Space

In intergalactic voids with hydrogen density ~1 atom/m³:
- Domains are large (order 10m? — needs calculation discounting neutrinos)
- Most volume is far from domain walls, filaments, worldlines
- Events (5-domain meetings) are extremely rare
- Very few R-processes → very little information generation → very
  little curvature → consistent with observed near-flatness of voids

The domain size in intergalactic space is a calculable quantity from
the matter density and the Screen Theory generation rate. This could
be a testable prediction.

### Connection to Annealing Picture

"Reality slowly anneals under the pressure of recorded history."
R-process Events are the 5-domain critical events of this annealing.
As the universe ages and history accumulates, the domain structure
becomes increasingly constrained — fewer and fewer configurations
are consistent with the full accumulated record. The annealing
drives toward lower informational entropy (more settled questions)
while generating more classical information (more settled outcomes).

This is the cosmological arrow of time in the domain picture.
